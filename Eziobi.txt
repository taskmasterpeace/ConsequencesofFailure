Kaiser Eziobi awoke with a start, the persistent honking of Lagos' early morning traffic invading his dreams. He rubbed his eyes and swung his legs over the edge of the bed, cursing the incessant noise that guaranteed he couldn't fall back asleep. The first light of day was beginning to creep across his bedroom walls, hinting at the sunrise that would soon light up the city.

He stood up and walked over to the small bathroom in his cramped apartment. As he splashed water on his face, he stared at his reflection: a dark-skinned man with cropped hair, tired eyes, and a tense jaw. Memories of a life once shared with his friend Justus threatened to cloud his mind, but he shook them away. It had been two years, and much had changed since then.

Returning to his bedroom, Kaiser knelt at the foot of his bed and clasped his hands together in a moment of attempted prayer. Although he'd lost faith in the higher power he'd been taught to revere as a child, he still found solace in the familiar ritual. The comforting repetition of prayers he’d whispered since youth steadied him in his chaotic world.

Today, Kaiser felt the need to cling to whatever semblance of stability he could muster. A heaviness weighed on him, a sense of unease that he couldn't shake. Fate had a habit of stirring when least expected, and Kaiser sensed that change was coming.

In his office at J&K Helicopters, Kaiser struggled to concentrate. Papers cluttered his desk, testament to his struggle to manage the administrative duties that had once belonged to Justus. A single picture hung on the wall – a snapshot of himself and the man he'd once called friend. He couldn't bear to take it down, a reminder of the bond they'd shared and the dream that had once been theirs.

Despite the challenges and the ghosts of the past, they had accomplished something together. J&K Helicopters was still operational, though clientele had become scarce. Today, there was just one scheduled flight at 10 am, transporting a Korean diplomat and his wife to Borno State – a tense region troubled by the activities of Akrahuhum, the notorious terrorist group known for forcing captures to renounce their religions beliefs at gunpoint.

Before the impending flight, however, an urgent message caught his attention. The designated pilot had called in sick, prompting Kaiser to respond that he'd handle the flight himself. It had been too long since he'd flown a helicopter, and despite everything, some part of him longed to take control of the skies once more.

The sun burned brightly overhead as Kaiser surveyed the helicopter,  checking the engine to ensure its readiness for the journey. His passengers arrived promptly, escorted in a luxurious Rolls Royce Phantom that looked out of place on the rough Nigerian roads. Kaiser frowned at the sight, suspecting it was the least of their ostentatious displays.

As the Korean couple strolled toward the helicopter, Kaiser took in their attire. The diplomat, clad in a tan suit, carried a shiny briefcase and wore a camera around his neck – a bold statement in a world where taking photographs could mean risking your life. His wife, dressed in a tight, glamorous dress and heels, clutched a small purse delicately in her hands. The pair seemed completely out of place in the dangerous landscape of Borno State, and Kaiser silently wondered what could be in that briefcase to warrant such a risk.

Silently, he watched as the helicopter's blades began to spin, whisking them away and toward a destination that promised more than met the eye.

Chapter 2:

As the helicopter ascended smoothly over the Nigerian landscape, Kaiser observed the couple from his pilot's seat. They whispered to one another, occasionally stealing glances at their precious briefcase, seemingly oblivious to the potential hazards that lay ahead. 

Touching down on the outskirts of Damboa, Kaiser stole a moment to take in the desolate environment, brutally scarred by the violence Akrahuhum's reign had inflicted. The Korean couple disembarked, and with their briefcase in hand, began walking down a dirt path leading into the heart of the town. Kaiser waited, suppressing his curiosity and hoping the exchange would go smoothly.

Hours passed, every moment stretching forbiddingly as Kaiser's anxiety grew. Looking for any distraction, he inspected the controls and instruments in the cockpit one more time, each dial and switch reassuringly functional. It was when he looked up that he saw a small convoy of camo jeeps, speeding along the dirt path toward the couple.

Kaiser kept his distance and observed, his heart quickening. The tension between the couple and the approaching terrorists carrying Akrahuhum's sigil was palpable even from where he was. Concern and fascination etched across his face as he tried to make sense of the unfolding events.

As the volatile exchange came to an end, the Korean couple began their sprint back to the helicopter, with chaos from the furious gunfire erupting behind them. Kaiser's well-calculated, steady character suddenly gave way to frantic desperation, as he fired up the engine and prepared for a hasty takeoff.

The horror of what happened next would forever change Kaiser's life. With the diplomat shot dead before reaching safety and the wife's anguished wails filling the air, Kaiser found himself forced to weigh potential heroics against survival. In a split-second decision, he helped the grieving wife into the helicopter and took off, the cacophony of gunfire and burning wreckage echoing in his ears.

Hovering above the chaos, smoke and dust below, Kaiser's mind raced. How did he end up in such a harrowing situation? Who were these people, and what was so important about that briefcase? As he flew back to Lagos with the traumatized widow by his side, Kaiser's life took on a new purpose: to uncover the truth, no matter the cost. Little did he know that this quest for answers would unlock a hidden world of intrigue, one in which he would become a key player, battling for control over the darkest secrets of their time.
